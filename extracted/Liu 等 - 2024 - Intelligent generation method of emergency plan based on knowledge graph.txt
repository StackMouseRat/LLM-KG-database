Abstract—The traditional emergency response based on static 
text is usually limited by the experience and ability of the 
responders, which is low in emergency efficiency and easy to 
cause omissions. In this study, a knowledge graph framework 
based on artificial intelligence technology is constructed by 
integrating multi-source data such as emergency regulation and 
monitoring. On the basis of this framework, an entity extraction 
method based on BERT-BILSTM-CRF hybrid model and a 
relation extraction method based on piecewise convolutional 
neural network (PCNN) and word segmentation technology are 
proposed. Then combined with Neo4j graph database, the 
semantic emergency plan knowledge base is constructed to 
realize the intelligent storage and visualization of emergency plan. 
Finally, the intelligent generating page of emergency plan is 
developed by using computer technology, and the feasibility of 
the research results is verified, which provides a new technical 
means for improving the efficiency of emergency response. 
Keywords-knowledge graph; artificial intelligence; entity 
recognition; relationship extraction; deep learning; 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Knowledge graph is a knowledge representation method 
proposed by Google in 2012, which can represent structured 
relationships between entities and is currently an important 
research direction in the field of artificial intelligence [1-3]. 
Knowledge graphs are developed on the basis of traditional 
knowledge bases, usually composed of "entity relationship 
entity" or "entity relationship attribute" triplets[4, 5]. Compared 
to traditional knowledge bases, knowledge graphs contain 
fewer semantics and rich instances, are flexible in form, and 
have higher scalability. In recent years, knowledge graph 
technology has attracted many applications in academia and 
industry, and various artificial intelligence related applications 
use it as prior knowledge to search for optimal solutions or 
encode real-world knowledge. 
At present, the emergency response work mainly relies on 
safety management personnel to manually search for 
emergency plan text files. Although keyword search methods 
can roughly search for the content of the plan text, the search 
results are usually large in content and scope, making it 
difficult to quickly find emergency response plans, resulting in 
untimely formulation of emergency response plans and 
reducing the efficiency of operation and maintenance 
management. To cope with the complex emergency response 
work, break through the traditional operational bottleneck of 
relying on manual experience for emergency response, and 
improve the intelligence level of operation and maintenance 
management, it is necessary to use knowledge graph 
technology to transform the scattered texts of emergency plans 
into interrelated knowledge. Through the construction of 
standardized, structured and semantic engineering emergency 
plan knowledge base, the efficient storage, visual display and 
quick retrieval of the emergency plan knowledge are realized, 
and the intelligent level of on-site emergency management and 
emergency disposal efficiency are improved. Therefore, 
studying the application methods of knowledge graphs in the 
field of emergency response has important theoretical 
significance and practical value for improving the efficiency 
of engineering operation and maintenance safety management. 
Based on this, this study considers constructing a large-scale 
semantic knowledge graph, transforming scattered text into 
interrelated knowledge, providing timely and effective 
auxiliary 
information 
for 
emergency 
decision-making, 
achieving intelligent generation of emergency plans, and 
improving the efficiency of safety management. 
II. KNOWLEDGE GRAPH FRAMEWORK  
The framework for constructing the knowledge graph of 
emergency plans is shown in Fig.1. Firstly, analyze all 
emergency plan text content and use a top-down approach to 
construct the pattern layer of the knowledge graph. Then, 
based on the pattern layer, unstructured text data such as 
inspection data, accident descriptions, and emergency plans 
are selected as objects. With the help of natural language 
processing techniques, including knowledge extraction, 
knowledge storage and visualization, and knowledge updates, 
a knowledge graph data layer is constructed.

III.  METHOD  
A. BERT-BiLSTM-CRF architecture 
As shown in Fig.2, the proposed model architecture 
consists of four main parts: (1) Preprocessing text data, 
including text cleaning, stop word removal, and entity 
annotation. (2) Introducing BERT as a vectorized feature 
representation method for pre trained text, it uses a 
bidirectional Transformer network structure as the encoder. (3) 
Using BiLSTM as a language model with long-term sequence 
information to generate semantic features of emergency plan 
texts, the results of character vectorization representation in 
the BERT layer are used as inputs to the BiLSTM layer. (4) 
Use CRF model and Viterbi algorithm for decoding, and 
output the final entity recognition result. 
 
Figure 2. Knowledge graph construction framework.  
B. Text preprocessing 
The original text data often contains various noise 
information that affects the accuracy of entity recognition. 
Therefore, before inputting the emergency plan text into the 
hybrid model, the following three steps need to be followed 
for text preprocessing: 
(1) Text cleaning refers to the removal of useless data such 
as spaces, punctuation marks, and letters that interfere with 
recognition performance during the training process. 
(2) Splitting characters, dividing the input text data into 
individual characters (E1, E2,..., EN), and then storing each 
individual character as a vocabulary, meaning that each 
character has a corresponding unique identifier (represented as 
[Character: ID]).

G. Knowledge storage and visualization 
A graph database is a non-relational database that 
represents data in nodes and edges, with a comprehensive 
query language and high query efficiency. This section uses 
the Neo4j graph database to achieve knowledge storage and 
visualization. 
Neo4j database is a high-performance NoSQL graphical 
database with advantages such as high availability, easy 
scalability, and fast retrieval. The Neo4j database is developed 
based on Java language and uses Cypher language for data 
queries and updates. Construct a knowledge graph based on 
the entities and relationships extracted from the triplets, where 
the entities correspond to nodes in the knowledge graph and 
the relationships corre-spond to edges in the knowledge graph. 
The process of building an emergency plan knowledge graph 
in Neo4j mainly involves three steps, as follows: (1) Import 
entity files, create entity nodes, and add a unique index 
(UNIQUE) to each entity; (2) Import relation-ship files, match 
relationships between nodes, and generate relationship edges; 
(3) Create a knowledge graph 
IV.  MODEL VALIDATION 
A. Experimental data and environment Settings 
Entity extraction is a key step in constructing a knowledge 
graph, and the accuracy of recognition results usually affects 
the accuracy of subsequent relationship extraction and 
knowledge storage. Therefore, analyzing and verifying it is 
crucial to ensure the accura te construction of a knowledge 
graph. The dataset used in the entity recognition verification 
process is divided into two parts: one part is 1000 engineering 
accidents that were self collected through network retrieval, 
including the time, cause, and location of the accidents; Part of 
it is the emergency plan system for a certain project. By using 
text pre-processing to remove non textual data and segmenting 
Chinese text into individual characters, the recognition 
efficiency of subsequent models is improved. Next, the BIO 
method is used for entity annotation. Randomly divide the 
dataset into training and testing sets in an 8:2 ratio, with a total 
of 1431 manually labeled entities. During the dataset 
partitioning process, the ratio of sample class distribution for 
each entity remains similar to the original ratio.

D. Knowledge graph application 
The emergency knowledge graph application module is 
based on the Windows 10 operating system, developed using 
Python 3.6 programming language, Django development 
framework, and Neo4j graph database. Among them, Django 
is an open source web application framework written in 
Python, which uses the MVT (Model View Template) 
development pattern to retrieve and visualize entity and 
relationship related subgraphs.

The emergency knowledge graph application module 
includes 
three 
functional 
pages: 
entity

relationship query, and plan retrieval. By entering the 
emergency plan text on the entity recognition page and 
clicking submit, the entity recognition results can be 
automatically generated and viewed. The relationship query 
page can query relationship information by entering entities. 
The emergency plan query page is shown in Fig.5. When the 
question "What is the emergency response method for flood 
disasters?" is entered, a corresponding emergency plan graph 
demonstration can be generated. Viewing the graph, it can be 
seen that flood disasters can lead to sudden accidents such as 
"overtopping of flood embankments", "local landslides on 
slopes", "instability at the foot of slopes", etc. Different 
accidents correspond to different disposal measures. When 
"instability at the foot of slopes" occurs, "throwing stones" can 
be adopted measures such as using geotextile bags to press 
feet. It can be seen that the plan retrieval provides a clear and 
systematic response plan for emergency personnel, which can 
effectively assist management personnel in making timely and 
effective emergency decisions.

V.   CONCLUSIONS 
This study proposes an intelligent generation method for 
emergency plans based on knowledge graphs, and verifies the 
effectiveness of the proposed entity recognition algorithm 
through engineering examples. Finally, rapid generation of 
emergency knowledge was achieved through the development 
of application modules. This provides intelligent means to 
improve the efficiency of emergency decision-making in 
engineering and ensure the safe operation of the project in a 
timely manner.
